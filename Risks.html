Risks

While working on this project, it is inevitable that we will face some risks. We performed a risk assessment to identify and address possible risks related to the development and use of Green Thumb.

General risks such as poor time management, hardware issues and communication breakdowns are a risk with any project. We will mitigate these general risks by following the group processes and communications plan as outlined later in this document. Additionally, we will catch up regularly with all team members to ensure effective time management and notify the team of any hardware issues so that we can quickly solve them and reduce the impact on the project.

Developmental Risk Assessment

Risk Probability of the risk Impact of this risk Risk Response Strategy
Lack of engineering experience Within our group just 1 member has experience engineering circuits similar to what is required of the probe. As such there would be a strong probability that we will be limited in what we can produce Potentially the probe could have issues with functionality, either completely or partially To respond to this risk, we will aim to produce a working prototype of the probe rather than a ready for market probe. Nikhil will use his engineering skills lead this task to the best of his abilities and team members will support him through user testing
Cybersecurity risks The probability of cyber related attacks will increase as the Green Thumb users increases and when the project has more exposure, and value Potential leak of user data and Green Thumb database assets As a response we will perform an investigation into how to secure our database and develop a cybersecurity strategy to protect the data contained within Green Thumb
Lack of app development experience No one in our team has built an application before. This will likely lead to issues with development of the application The team will not be able to produce a proper, functioning application Producing an entire app with our skills is out of scope so we will develop a prototype in figma. This way we can demonstrate the functionality of the app to developers who can then build the app to get it ready for market.
Lack of data science and machine learning expertise As first time developers, the team has no experience with building artificial intelligence systems. This is an unavoidable risk. Without AI, the software will not function as intended It will be out of scope to build the proposed AI for Green Thumb, instead, we will develop an AI strategy to be implemented by an AI specialist. We will research possible platforms and propose machine learning models as a guide for the specialists to follow.
Lack of database programming skills No members of our team have built a database before. The probability of creating a database fit for the purpose of the project will not likely happen. The application relies on the database of information about plant species. Without a proper running database the application will not work as intended We will develop a concept for a database that describes the data to be collected and the relationships between databases. We will make some attempts to write the basic lines of code which could then be passed off to a programmer to finalise and deploy.
App may not be IOS compatible As complex iOS applications could potentially require a subscription to access development tools it will be likely that we will not be able to create the prototype to be iOS compatible. The application prototype cannot be tested and potentially will not be launched on iOS for the time being. To bypass this problem, the team will develop the app using the Blynk Platform. This will allow us to test and launch on both Android and iOS devices.
All the components of the app (AI, Probe, database) etc may not integrate There is always a possibility that hardware components will have compatibility issues. It is however unlikely as all the parts we will be using will be verified to be compatible with the “Arduino ecosystem”. This could potentially render the entire system unusable, or make certain features unusable depending on which incompatibilities are present. To address potential incompatibilities, we will ensure all the parts are compatible with one another and then test the hardware. We will be switching out anything that may cause an issue.



Developmental Risk Assessment

Risk Probability of the risk Impact of this risk Risk Response Strategy
The probe contains small parts which may be a choking hazard The probability of this risk is low. Assuming the plant is accessible by a child the probe will still be embedded within the soil of the plant. It is unlikely for the probe to be ingested when it is in use. Child may suffocate from swallowing parts of the device. Packaging will adequately labelled with warnings about choking hazards so parents are informed and can take precautionary steps
Probe may be damaged by the soil environment The DH22 temperature and moisture sensor has a strong chance of being infiltrated by soil. Can result in permanent damage to the probe rendering it unusable. Replace the DH22 sensor with a capacitive moisture sensor and a DS18B20 temperature sensor which have a much lower probability of becoming damaged by soil
Misuse of the app It would be probable for users to be able to get the app before getting the probe. As it would be advertised on Application Marketplaces such as Google Play. The app without the probe will render the Green Thumb system ineffective. We will not advertise the app on widely public app marketplaces. Instead we will have the application obtainable via our own Website through the QR code in the Probe packaging.
Encountering new species of plant As there are many different species of plant life, it would be impossible to assume that the Green Thumb database has information on all of them, this means that there can be a fair chance that specific plant life will not be registered. The plant will not be identified by the probe or application, or will identify it as another plant that is similar. As far as the strategy for launch, Green Thumb will have data on most common household species to reduce the likelihood of this risk. Eventually, the machine learning model will become intelligent enough to detect new species and gather the correct data to provide insights.
Bugs/technical difficulties in the app The application will probably have bugs as every application is expected to have some problems. Minor bugs can impact the user experience slightly however, depending on the severity of the bug, some aspects of the app may not operate as expected or crash the application making it unusable. We will add a support function to the app to allow users to get in touch and report issues.An automatic crash report system will be added to allow the user to send crash reports to Green Thumb if the application crashes. At launch we will ensure the application will be tested thoroughly so we can avoid as many bugs as possible.
Connectivity issues The probe relies on wifi to operate so the probability of this risk would be based on how stable internet connection is. It should also be noted that some plants in a garden environment may not be in range of Wi-Fi. The app and probe will not be able to operate sufficiently Advertising and marketing campaigns must emphasize the probe and apps reliance on wifi connectivity in order to be able to perform correctly. A screen on the probe may be a solution in notifying users if there is a stable connection.
