Green Thumb

project Plan

Green Thumb is a mobile app that tracks the health of indoor plants and diagnoses diseases. The app will be accompanied by a soil probe that allows users to measure the essential variables to plant health - temperature, moisture and soil pH levels. Using these measurements, the Green Thumb app will send alerts when a plant requires assistance such as watering or fertilisation. These measurements are stored within plant specific profiles and over time, the app will interpret the data to provide powerful insights to educate users. These insights will create a long term culture of plant growth and disease prevention.

If these disease preventative measures fail and a user begins to notice a decline in plant health, Green Thumb’s diagnostic tools will inspect photos and the probe data to determine the cause. Powered by ML (Machine Learning) and APIs (Application Programming Interfaces), the Green Thumb AI will be able to accurately diagnose disease and suggest solutions to restore the plant back to health. Green Thumb will take the guesswork out of gardening, preventing plant death and empowering even the most novice plant parent with the tools to grow their indoor jungle.   

Motivation

As we find ourselves home-office bound while the pandemic rages on, man​​y of us are looking to update our space and bring the outdoors in with the addition of plants. As well as looking beautiful, indoor plants work to purify the air we breathe and may even increase our productivity at work (Bringslimark, T., Hartig, T. & Grindal Patil, G. 2007). There is no better time to build your plant collection and Green Thumb will ensure plant success.

Traditionally, when entering the gardening world, many plant lives are lost, and money wasted as we try to build our gardening skills and learn what not to do. Beginners are often unsure about how to care for their plants and online forums seem to cause more confusion than clarity. Green Thumb is a one-stop-shop for beginners, providing them with tailored information about their specific species of plant by collecting real-time data and providing thorough AI-powered analysis.

Considering the current top 10 technology trends, as shown in figure 1, it is clear that Green Thumb taps into a number of these growing trends. With the trend of smart spaces and the growing demand for connectivity to the Internet of Things (IoT), it is evident that more and more people are looking to connect their everyday objects to the network (Panetta, K., 2018). Smart technology has already hit the gardening world with the recent development of smart pots. These pots track plant health through temperature and moisture sensors built into a plant pot that then alerts the user when to water their plant. Green Thumb will follow this smart technology trend but aims to offer a more affordable and portable solution to consumers gardening needs.

Furthermore, now more than ever is there a demand for AI technology. Gartner suggests that AI is already in virtually every existing technology today (Panetta, K., 2018) and this remains true for Green Thumb. The majority of consumers have already embraced AI, with most carrying digital assistants in their pocket via their smartphone. AI is able to go further than historical data alone, providing deeper insights into what has already occurred (Clark, S., 2020). In the case of solving gardeners' problems, this is an extremely valuable tool, as often it can be difficult to diagnose plant problems based on face value alone - a deeper investigation is required that may be outside of the skillset of the everyday gardener.

Landscape

A plant health app is not an entirely unique idea with 100s available to download, however what makes Green Thumb innovative is the creative use of technology to solve the problems users experience with the existing plant health solutions on the market.

We performed an analysis of the current leading plant health apps on the market to understand their functionalities and value for money. ​ 

From our market research it is evident that while a lot of apps offer species identification and some plant care tips, only a small percentage offer disease diagnosis. Disease diagnosis is a primary component of Green Thumb and sets us apart from other products on the market. We have identified PictureThis as our biggest competitor as it offers the key functions of the Green Thumb app and is rated 4.5/5 with over 24 thousand ratings on the Apple App Store. PictureThis uses AI to allow users to not only identify new plant species, but also diagnose plant problems through photos and get growing advice via a chat bot (Glority Global Group Ltd., N.D). PictureThis is a powerful educational tool but falls short of Green Thumb as it doesn’t offer any solutions for measuring soil health which is also a primary factor in accurately diagnosing plant disease. The American Phytopathological Society (APS) suggests that when diagnosing a plant disease, it is essential to observe soil pH as well as climatic factors such as temperature (Riley, B., Williamson, M. & Maloy, O., 2002). By understanding all the factors affecting plant health, Green Thumb will be able to compete against PictureThis’ popularity on the market while boasting increased functionalities and a higher diagnosis accuracy.

While a lot of apps exist on the market with similar functionalities, we were unable to identify any that combined image recognition AI with plant soil data collected from sensors. During this search however, we discovered the existence of smart pot technology. Smart pots are plant pots with built in sensors to detect soil moisture and temperature. Using this data, the pot can alert users when the plant requires water or is too hot/cold. This is a relatively new technology, recently developed by Mu Designs and as such they are currently the only competitor that we could identify in the smart pot landscape. There is a clear market for this type of technology, with Mu Designs raising over half a million dollars from backers in their 2019/20 Indiegogo campaign (Muller, V. N.D). We analysed Green Thumb against Lua to compare functionalities and explore how Green Thumb could compete in this market. 

The biggest drawback we identified in Lua is that all the technology is built into a single pot that can only house one plant. Considering the price of the pot, consumers are faced with a significant financial roadblock if they wish to track the health of multiple plants throughout their home. Green Thumb solves this problem by integrating the functionalities of a smart pot into a handheld probe that can be inserted into the soil of multiple plants. Green Thumb differentiates itself further by not only tracking soil moisture and temperature but also pH. Although neglected in Lua’s design, soil pH is an essential factor to plant health as it affects the amount of nutrients available to plants (Queensland Government, 2013). By measuring moisture, temperature and pH, Green Thumb is able to give a much more detailed overview of plant health and give users suggestions for how to keep their plants healthy.

To summarise, while a lot of technology already exists in the plant health landscape, Green Thumb will be a major competitor due to it’s affordable price point, disease diagnosis tool and ability to analyse real time data to provide intelligent insights into plant health.
