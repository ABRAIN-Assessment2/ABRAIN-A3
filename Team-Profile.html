Team Personality Profile

In terms of our Myer Briggs Personality test results, our group actually has a lot of similar personality types. Most of our members fall under the category of ‘diplomat’ with a mix of ‘advocates’ (Bridget, Adrian and Arvee), ‘campaigner’ (Nikhil) and a ‘protagonist’ (Renee). Diplomats, especially advocates, make great team players. Additionally having a campaigner in our group is a benefit as they are known for their strong communication skills. Having so many diplomatic personality types will be an advantage for this assignment as we mostly take a similar approach to the world around us. However we should be mindful that diplomats tend to be more sensitive to criticism, so we should be sure to provide constructive criticism to one another.

Idris, unlike the rest of the group members, falls into the ‘explorer’ category, as an ‘adventurer’. Adventurers are characterised by their curiosity, imagination and passion, which is good to help power ideas and create interest within the group. Adventurers are also prone to being fiercely independent, unpredictable and easily stressed, which are qualities that could potentially disrupt productivity within the group.

When considering our learning styles, we are fortunate to have similar learning styles. Bridget, Adrian and Nik are all visual learners meaning they learn best through visual representations. Additionally, Renee has a multimodal learning preference, which similar to visual learners, means that she prefers to learn through diagrams, graphs and other visual materials. Arvee is the outlier in terms of learning style, he is a pragmatic learner meaning he needs to see how to put the learning into practice in the real world. When working as a team, it is fortunate to have so many members with the same learning style, we will be able to effectively communicate through visual materials. It will be important for each team member to consider Arvee’s needs as a pragmatic learning, and provide him with practical examples of information or possibly even case studies.
