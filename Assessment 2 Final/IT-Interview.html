Interview Recording

with Suwa Kuma by the team.

Suwa Kuma

Suwa is a Digital Channel Manager for Commonwealth Bank (CBA). At CBA, Suwa is responsible for leading strategic projects on the banks digital channels - which include the CommBank App, public website, NetBank (online banking portal), Ceba (AI chatbot) and open banking.

Find out more via Suwa's LinkedIn

Open banking is a government legislation, what it wants to do is bring all that data together so there is a "One Stop Shop" for the customer to get a view of all their banking information, so that could be transaction level or also customer level as well."

Interview Reflection

When considering a career in IT, finance and banking are not necessarily the industries that one would expect to to find work within. Our interview with Suwa was eye opening and gave us a strong understanding of the far reaching applications of IT across any industry.

Suwa’s role is centred around project management and delivery, which is an important skill for us to build upon if we wish to seek a career in IT. At Commonwealth bank, an agile project management approach is employed meaning that when they deliver a project, such as a new app function, the work does not stop at launch. Rather, they continue to reiterate and are constantly working to optimize the tool. This agile project delivery method is a common process in IT related projects, so it was interesting to learn more about the process and how we can build our skills in this area.

Suwa helped us to understand that when building new technology, it is important to consider people from all walks of life. When working on our project idea in assignment 3, we should consider how the technology would be received by different audiences - younger, older etc. Suwa emphasised the importance of getting feedback from your audience, so this is a tool we will use to optimize our project idea in assignment 3.

Suwa described the essence of the IT Industry as using technology to help people solve problems. It was incredibly interesting to learn about how Commonwealth Bank has used technology to enhance the customer experience and solve their pain points. He has left us with a newfound sense of curiosity into how we can use technology to solve problems across all industries and areas of the globe.
